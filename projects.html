Budget Constrained Machine Learning Optimizations
Assistant Prof. Sumit Kalra, IIT Jodhpur Autumn 2019

Neural networks have maded an undeniable impact in advancing the state-of-art for many fundamental computer vision tasks. Much of these performance improvements were enabled by ever increasing amount of labelled data and innovations in training architecture.
However, as training dataset continues to grow in size, we argue that an additional limiting factor is resource constraints for training. Conservative prognostications of dataset sizes particularly for practical endeavours such as self-driving cars, assistive medical robots and medical analysis. Planning efforts will be more and more crucial, because in the limit, it might not even be practical to visit every training example before running out of resources. Resources constraint can be space constraint or compute power constraint.

I studied various optimizations techniques that can be used for reducing the training time. I benchmarked various neural network to figure out those bottlenecks. One of method implemented was gradient checkpointing with a default heuristics, which allows computer with low memory to train neural networks effeciently. And also I have implemented map reduce technique for training deep neural networks and achieved the same results with 5% tolerance. The map reduce technique can be used to give a proof-of-concept while desinging a neural network.

Future Scope: Accuracy of training a neural network using map reduce can be further improved by using genetic algorithm or swarm particle optimization like techniques. And a generic framework can be developed for tenserflow 2.x for gradient checkpointing.

Generative Face Completion
Associate Prof. Gaurav Harit, IIT Jodhpur Febuary - April 2019

Inpainting, a common image processing operation, aims to fill the missing regions of an image with synthesized components. It can prove beneficial to a multitude of applications ranging from restoration of damaged paintings and photographs to replacement of selected objects in images. Generating semantically valid pixels to fill sections of a human face can be particularly challenging, due to the presence of large variations in appearance. A copy and paste strategy may perform well for background completion, but may end up producing invalid results when used to complete facial images. A recently developed technology that can prove to be useful in such situations, is Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN). Using generative networks for face completion gives promising results.

We implemented a research paper on Generative Adversarial Network consisting of 2 discriminators ie. one local and one global to handle the task of impainting. We achieved realistic face images.
